StudIA

This project is a comprehensive academic resource management platform designed to function similarly to Google Drive, but with advanced features tailored for academic use. Built using Quarkus, a modern, lightweight Java framework, along with Python and Flask for backend development, the system integrates a Large Language Model (LLM) that applies Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) techniques. The Chess Big dataset is explored and analyzed using Pandas to gain insights from a large collection of game moves. EEG Research

This thesis explores the challenge of analysing temporal affective consistency through EEG signals, focusing on the stable association between emotional states and physiological responses over time. Applying a crowdsourcing approach to collect diverse physiological responses and integrating the acquisition of brain signals using a brainsourcing methodology, the study examines the temporal dimension of EEG signals. Previous research has often overlooked the importance of the temporal structure of the sequence, assuming that all segments have the same weight. In contrast, this paper proposes novel methods for quantifying affective consistency using data-agnostic heuristics, data-aware techniques and learning-based approaches. These methods introduce weighted consistency schemes to improve emotion recognition models by assessing whether temporal consistency can be meaningful and independent of video content. Experimental evaluation on EEG DEAP and MAHNOB datasets reveals that learning-based consistency weighting methods outperform traditional uniform weighting, demonstrating greater generalisability across different subjects. While the results are promising, there is a need to refine feature selection and understand the limitations of the data-driven methods. This research paves the way towards more robust EEG-based emotion recognition systems, with applications in human-computer interaction, mental health, affective video annotation and education.
